Five grams sounds like nothing. Shaving the last gram off OmniBuds Pro 3 took
eighteen months and a lot of dead ends. Here's the honest version of the build.

## Simulating the chamber

Before a single prototype, we ran thousands of acoustic chamber simulations.
The goal: a port geometry that gives low-end weight without a bulky housing.
Most candidates looked great in the model and rattled in the hand.

## The trade-off nobody mentions

Smaller housing means a smaller battery. Every gram we removed cost us minutes
of playback. The final design is a compromise we're proud of — **compact enough
to forget you're wearing it, with a charge that survives a working day.**

## Real-world commutes

Lab data is necessary but never sufficient. We tested on actual commutes —
wind, sweat, the yank of a coat sleeve. Half our "finished" designs failed the
sleeve test. So we went back.

Engineering is mostly the dead ends you don't ship. The five grams are what's left.
