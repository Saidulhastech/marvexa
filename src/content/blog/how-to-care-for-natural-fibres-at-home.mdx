## Read the Fibre, Not Just the Label

Care labels are a starting point, but understanding the fibre tells you more. Each natural material has a temperament — learn it once and you'll care for every garment of that type for life.

## Wool and Cashmere

Wash sparingly, in cool water with a gentle wool detergent, and never wring. Press water out flat and dry on a towel away from direct heat. Between wears, let knits rest a day to recover their shape, and store them folded — never hung — to avoid shoulder distortion.

## Linen and Cotton

These are the workhorses and the most forgiving. Cool machine washes keep them fresh; line-drying preserves the fibres and softens the hand over time. Embrace linen's natural creasing rather than fighting it — it's part of the character.

## Silk

Hand-wash in cool water with a drop of mild detergent, or dry-clean for structured pieces. Keep silk out of direct sunlight, which fades it quickly, and iron on low while slightly damp.

## Store It Right

The most common damage happens in the wardrobe, not the wash. Use cedar rather than mothballs, give garments room to breathe, and keep everything clean before long storage — moths are drawn to traces of skin and food, not the fabric itself.
