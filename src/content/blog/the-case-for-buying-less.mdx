## The Cost of More

Every garment you own carries a hidden tax: the space it takes, the decision it adds each morning, the laundry it generates. A fuller wardrobe rarely means more to wear — it usually means more to wade through. Buying less isn't deprivation; it's the removal of friction.

## Cost Per Wear, Not Cost Per Item

The cheapest garment is the one you wear two hundred times, not the one with the lowest price tag. A well-made coat at a higher price, worn for years, costs pennies per wear. A bargain that pills after a season costs far more. Shift the question from "what does this cost?" to "what will this cost me each time I wear it?"

## Wear It Out, Properly

Buying less only works if you wear what you have fully. Repair the loose button. Re-sole the boots. Re-wear the outfit that worked last week. The most sustainable, and most stylish, wardrobe is one whose pieces show the quiet evidence of being genuinely used.

## Build Slowly

Resist filling gaps instantly. Live with a small, considered wardrobe and let real needs reveal themselves over weeks. The pieces you add deliberately, in answer to an actual gap, are the ones that stay.
