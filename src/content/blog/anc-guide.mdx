Most noise-cancellation marketing is written for a quiet room. Real life is a
Boeing 737 at cruise, a subway car with steel wheels on steel rail, and the
colleague two desks over on a speakerphone. We tested 24 headphones across all
three and the gap between the spec sheet and the seat was wide.

## What actually matters

The number brands love to quote — "up to 40dB of reduction" — is measured at a
single frequency in a lab. The low rumble of a jet engine sits where most ANC
works well. The human voice does not. If your commute is people, not engines,
chase **mid-frequency attenuation and a good seal**, not the headline dB.

## The seal beats the chip

In every test, fit mattered more than the processor. A mid-range set with the
right ear tips out-cancelled a flagship that didn't seal. Spend the ten minutes
to size your tips before you spend on a new pair.

## What we'd buy

- **Flights:** over-ear, strong low-end ANC, long battery.
- **Commute:** in-ear with great passive isolation first, ANC second.
- **Office:** transparency mode you'll actually leave on.

The honest version: the best ANC is the pair you'll wear correctly, every day.
