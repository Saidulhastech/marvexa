## A Capsule Is a Method, Not a Price

A capsule wardrobe is simply a small set of pieces that all work together. Nothing about that requires a large budget — it requires a clear plan. Done well, a capsule actually costs less over time because every purchase is deliberate and every piece gets worn.

## Start with What You Own

Before buying anything, lay out everything you already wear regularly. Most people discover their real capsule is already hiding in their wardrobe, buried under pieces they never reach for. Identify the items that earn their place, and you'll see the gaps clearly.

## Prioritise the Heavy Lifters

Put your money where the wear is. The trouser, the knit, the outerwear layer — the pieces you'll use several times a week — justify the most investment. Save on the occasional pieces; they're worn rarely and don't need to survive hundreds of washes.

## Fill Gaps Slowly

A capsule is built over months, not in a single weekend haul. Add one considered piece at a time, in answer to a real gap, and watch how quickly a small, cohesive wardrobe outperforms a large, chaotic one — on any budget.
