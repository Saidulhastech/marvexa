A treadmill review tells you almost nothing. I took the Omnix Watch Ultra into
three weeks of real marathon training — pre-dawn long runs, interval days, and
the kind of weather that fogs a screen.

## GPS that holds the line

Under tree cover and beside tall buildings, most watches wander. The Ultra's
dual-band GPS stayed within a stride of my measured loop on all but one run.
For pace work, that consistency matters more than peak accuracy.

## Heart rate, honestly

Wrist optical sensors struggle in the cold and during fast cadence changes. The
Ultra lagged a chest strap by a few seconds on intervals — fine for easy runs,
worth a strap for serious threshold work.

## Battery under real load

With GPS and continuous HR on, I got **roughly 18 hours** of active tracking —
enough for a marathon with margin, not enough to forget the charger on a
multi-day trip.

It's not magic. It's a genuinely good training watch that respects your data.
