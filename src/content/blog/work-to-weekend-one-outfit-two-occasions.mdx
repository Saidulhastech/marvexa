## Dress the Core, Swap the Edges

The secret to a piece that works morning to evening is a stable core and changeable edges. Keep the trouser and shirt constant; change the layer, the shoe, and one accessory. Two small swaps shift the whole register without a trip home.

## The Day-to-Night Swaps

- Trade the blazer for a soft knit, or the other way around
- Swap leather loafers for a clean sneaker, or vice versa
- Untuck the shirt, or add a belt to sharpen it
- Drop the work bag for something smaller in the evening

## Choose Pieces That Translate

Some garments simply move better between contexts. A dark straight-leg trouser, a fine knit, an unstructured blazer — none of them shout "office," so none of them feel out of place at dinner. Build around pieces with this quiet adaptability and the swaps become almost automatic.

## Plan It Once

Set up two or three reliable day-to-night combinations and you'll never improvise under pressure. The point isn't more clothes — it's getting far more out of the few that already earn their place.
